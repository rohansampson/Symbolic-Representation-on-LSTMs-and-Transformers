SSTS

Symbolic Search in Time Series
This is a site to help you in discovering a new technique for pattern search in time series. We aim for something more intuitive and faster to test in simple problems of time series analysis. Scroll down for more information, and experience this novel way of searching patterns in time series.

ABOUT

This tools aims in reducing the gap between your reasoning and the processing tasks needed for query search in time series, turning this into a general concept that could be extended to researchers, practitioners, medical specialists and data scientists who need more intuitive methods to retrieve knowledge from data. The process uses symbols in several steps, which are presented below.

Processing

Load and prepare the signal for the Symbolic Connotation step. With a set of symbols, apply typical and specific signal processing methods, such as filters, normalization, modulus, among others...

Symbolic Connotation

Convert the signal into a symbolic representation, as a sequence of symbols that represent the major characteristics of the signal.

Search

Write a string that matches the pattern that you are looking for and the sequence of symbols of the symbolic signal. This string will be interpreted as a regular expression.

EXAMPLES

Plateau

Detect the beginning and ending of a plateau that corresponds to a rotation above 60 degrees.

Step Detection

Detect the instants in time when a right or left heel floor contact is achieved during normal straight walk. Since the sensor is located on the right pocket, global minima will indicate the right heel contact and local minima will correspond to left heel contact.

Systolic Phase Detection

The dicrotic notch corresponds to the physiological event of the aortical valve closure, which triggers the increase of the aortic pressure and signifies the end of the systolic phase. Segment the systolic phase of the arterial blood pressure wave.

ECG peak detetor

Detect all the major peaks from the record.

Straight Line Tracking

The automatic detection of features, such straight walks and turns can be used to look for trajectory anomalies.

Stable Lifting Detection

Since the first 5 seconds of the lifting exercise are unstable, the problem involves detecting the first stable lifting step, which occurs approximately 5 seconds after the beginning of the exercise
